Herbal Support for Your BMI Category

This guide offers Traditional Chinese Medicine herbal insight based on your BMI result. It aims to provide gentle, balanced support—not medical advice. Please consult a qualified practitioner before beginning any herbal regimen.

BMI < 18.5 — Underweight

TCM Patterns: Blood Deficiency, Yin Deficiency
Elemental Type: Wood / Fire

Herbs:

  • 當歸 (Dāng Guī) – nourishes blood
  • 熟地黃 (Shú Dì Huáng) – tonifies blood and yin
  • 白芍 (Bái Sháo) – nourishes blood, preserves yin
  • 黃耆 (Huáng Qí) – strengthens Qi to support blood

Supportive Foods: beets, black sesame, dates, leafy greens, millet, oats, bone broth

Contraindications: Avoid cold/raw and overly spicy foods

BMI 18.5–24.9 — Healthy

TCM Patterns: Balanced Qi and Blood
Elemental Type: Earth

  • 山藥 (Shān Yào), 黨參 (Dǎng Shēn), 白朮 (Bái Zhú)

Supportive Foods: sweet potatoes, rice, barley, ginger, cooked vegetables

Contraindications: Avoid greasy, rich, or overly processed foods

BMI 25–29.9 — Overweight

TCM Patterns: Dampness, Spleen Qi Deficiency
Elemental Type: Earth / Metal

  • 茯苓 (Fú Líng), 陳皮 (Chén Pí), 白朮 (Bái Zhú), 藿香 (Huò Xiāng)

Supportive Foods: lentils, adzuki beans, pumpkin, barley, leeks, ginger tea

Contraindications: Avoid sugar, dairy, cold/raw and flour-heavy foods

BMI 30–34.9 — Obesity Class I

TCM Patterns: Damp-Phlegm, Qi Stagnation
Elemental Type: Earth / Metal

  • 半夏 (Bàn Xià), 厚朴 (Hòu Pò), 枳實 (Zhǐ Shí), 陳皮 (Chén Pí)

Supportive Foods: daikon radish, mung beans, celery, green tea

Contraindications: Avoid fried food, excess meat and cheese, cold/raw intake

BMI 35–39.9 — Obesity Class II

TCM Patterns: Damp-Heat, Liver Qi Constraint
Elemental Type: Wood / Earth

  • 黃連 (Huáng Lián), 柴胡 (Chái Hú), 茵陳蒿 (Yīn Chén Hāo), 澤瀉 (Zé Xiè)

Supportive Foods: bitter greens, cucumber, chrysanthemum tea, turmeric

Contraindications: Avoid alcohol, spicy, greasy, and sugar-heavy foods

BMI ≥ 40 — Obesity Class III

TCM Patterns: Phlegm-Fire, Toxic Heat, Spleen Collapse
Elemental Type: Fire / Earth

  • 龍膽草 (Lóng Dǎn Cǎo), 黃芩 (Huáng Qín), 大黃 (Dà Huáng), 黨參 (Dǎng Shēn)

Supportive Foods: bitter melon, lotus root, wheatgrass, daikon, cabbage

Contraindications: Avoid sugar, alcohol, processed foods, and fried meals
