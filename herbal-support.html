Herbal Support for Your BMI Category

This guide offers Traditional Chinese Medicine herbal insight based on your BMI result. It aims to provide gentle, balanced support—not medical advice. Please consult a qualified practitioner before beginning any herbal regimen.

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

For those in the underweight range, TCM often sees Spleen Qi deficiency or weak digestion. Herbs that gently strengthen and nourish include:

  • Codonopsis (Dang Shen): Tonifies Qi and improves energy levels.
  • Hawthorn fruit (Shan Zha): Promotes digestion and appetite, especially for fatty foods.
  • Licorice root (Gan Cao): Harmonizes herbal formulas and supports Spleen health.

Consider combining these in a mild tonic—Codonopsis, Hawthorn, and Licorice—often used in TCM to support appetite and digestion.

Healthy Weight (BMI 18.5–24.9)

Maintaining this range often aligns with good Qi flow and balanced metabolism. Herbs that support vitality include:

  • American ginseng (Xi Yang Shen): Nourishes Qi and supports stamina.
  • Green tea (Lu Cha): Mild metabolic support and antioxidant benefits.
  • Jujube date (Da Zao): Gently harmonizes and calms, helpful in dietary balance.

These are supportive herbs, ideal for daily use to promote overall wellness and energy balance.

Overweight (BMI 25–29.9)

This range may indicate Dampness accumulation or reduced circulation within TCM principles. Herbs that help invigorate and regulate include:

  • Cinnamon twig (Gui Zhi): Warms the channels and promotes movement.
  • Lotus leaf (He Ye): Clears and transforms dampness.
  • Poria mushroom (Fu Ling): Supports fluid balance and digestive function.

These herbs are often combined in teas designed to support dampness transformation and light circulation support.

Obesity Class I (BMI 30–34.9)

Higher BMI levels may correspond to internal heat, phlegm-dampness, or blood stasis in TCM. Supportive herbs include:

  • Curcumin (Jiang Huang/Turmeric): Anti-inflammatory and circulatory support.
  • Red peony root (Chi Shao): Helps move stagnant blood.
  • Hawthorn leaf & flower (Shan Zha Ye): Supports digestion and circulation.

These herbs are often used in gentle circulatory and metabolic tonic formulas.

Obesity Class II–III (BMI ≥ 35)

This higher range may involve chronic inflammation, stagnant Qi, and metabolic stress. Consider these supportive herbs:

  • Turmeric (Jiang Huang) with Black Pepper: Enhances absorption; supports inflammation pathways.
  • Green tea extract (Lu Cha concentrated): Supports healthy metabolism.
  • Ginger (Sheng Jiang): Promotes Qi movement and digestive harmony.

These herbs should ideally be part of a personalized herbal formula—especially at higher BMI levels, professional guidance is recommended.

Note: These are traditional insights, not medical directives. Formulations are best chosen under professional TCM guidance.
